#3 Deadly Webcap

Deadly Webcap

"Tired of having the same old kidneys?"

Dangers

Ranked number three for most dangerous mushroom is the deadly webcap! This mushroom can poison simply by tasting, and results in kidney failure within weeks, leading to death! Even better, there is not yet an identified cure, so the only solution to not dying is a kidney transplant. However, the real danger for this fungi is its high rate of misdiagnosis. Since the symptoms arrive very slowly(unlike most poisonous mushrooms, whose effects may appear within a day) and are similar to that of a flu, the deadly webcap’s toxins are usually left mistreated, allowing them to to kill it’s victim!

Identification

The deadly webcap has a brownish-orange cap and crowded yellow gills(turns brown as spores mature). It has a diameter of about 4cm to 8cm. The surface of the mushroom is described to be rough(though touching the mushroom for confirmation is not recommended).






#2 Destroying Angel

Destroying Angel

"Your friendly, neighbourhood murderer"

Dangers

Judging by its name, you can already tell that the destroying angel isn’t exactly a harmless meal. But of course, someone had go and eat it. In fact, this mushroom is responsible for a vast portion of deaths caused in mushroom consumption. This is beacuse it is often mistakened for other mushroom types, such as the harmless white button, or common meadow mushrooms. At any rate, any consumers will quickly find out that the destroying angel was probably not what they had intended to eat, as symptoms such as delirium, spasms, throwing up, occur within the next 5 to 24 hours. Not to mention, these symptoms usually fade after a few hours, making the victim think that they are recovering when in reality, they are dying on the inside. Nice one, destroying angels. Eventually, the toxins lead to liver and kidney failure, with death quickly following.

Identification

Destroying angels are pure white, hence it’s name, "angels". They have very rounded, egg-shaped caps in their early stages, which expand outwards as the mushroom matures, though it still retains much of it’s dome structure. It has crowded gills and uaually, a curved stem.






#1 Death cap

Deathcap

"Apparently it tastes good"

Dangers

At long last, the winner of the most murderous and deadly mushroom goes to... the DEATH CAP!!! This is contributed to its qualities as an incredibly efficent killer, with a death rate of over 50% in 6 to 12 hours! The secret? Toxins from the death cap literally destroy cells, as it makes it’s way to the livers and kidneys, stopping their functions(like the destroying angel). However, the death cap also throws in a bonus of the central nervous system soon after, for extra damage points.

As if all of this wasn’t enough, the death cap leads other mushroom species in another battle; globalization. Death caps are mycorrhiza mushrooms, meaning they form a mutualistic bond between their host tree, and therefore are attached to its roots. Through the influence of helpful humans, who love to bring pretty plants with them as they travel, many death caps happily immigrated along with their hosts from their home continent of Europe to settle in North America. Apparently, it’s very nice in North America, because the mushrooms quickly established themselves in the ecosystem, though scientists aren’t yet sure of their influence. In any case, this opportunity to kill people from around the world grants them the number one spot on the list of dangerous mushroom species!

Identification

Death caps have a yellow dome cap when young, which opens to a flat white circle with yellow/green tints when matured. Like most other poisonous mushrooms, they also have crowded gills. They are safe to touch, with a somewhat sticky cap, as the toxins are only absorbed through injestion. The most defining feature of the death cap, however, is its large white volva.
